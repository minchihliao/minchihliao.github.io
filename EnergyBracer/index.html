Project Overview

The Energy Bracer project integrates virtual reality and tactile feedback technologies to create an immersive gaming experience that simulates temperature changes through a phenomenon called Thermal Diffusion. This technology was developed during my master's research and is central to enhancing sensory feedback in VR environments.

Objective

  • Innovation in VR
      Develop a VR system that uses tactile feedback to enhance the gaming experience by simulating temperature changes.
  • Research Application
      Apply findings from the study of human sensory responses to electrical and thermal stimuli in a practical VR application.

    • Technologies Used

  • Unity and C#: For VR game development.
  • HTC Vive: To provide an immersive VR experience.
  • Bluetooth Technology: For real-time device connectivity and feedback.

    • Technical Details

  • Device Engineering
      The Energy Bracer is equipped with electrical and temperature stimulators. We uses minimal electrical currents to stimulate thermal diffusion, calibrated to user comfort based on our research findings.
  • Feedback Optimization
      Provide the best thermal diffusion effect based on user survey to ensure an engaging and realistic experience.

    • Challenges and Solutions

  • Interdisciplinary Research
      The project required a deep dive into human physiology to effectively integrate sensory feedback in VR. I expanded my knowledge base beyond my primary field of study, demonstrating my ability to tackle interdisciplinary challenges.
  • Sensory Calibration
      Initial tests revealed that the temperature sensations induced by the Thermal Diffusion were not consistently perceivable by all users. To address this, I consulted extensive research on human thermal perception and conducted user feedback surveys to determine the most effective temperature ranges and electrical current settings for noticeable yet comfortable sensory feedback

    • Concrete Results

  • Enhanced Player Immersion
      Players reported a significantly enhanced feeling of immersion and realism due to the tactile feedback provided by the Energy Bracer.
  • Academic Contribution
      The project formed the basis of my master's thesis and contributed new insights into the application of tactile feedback technology in virtual reality.

    • My Role and Contributions

  • Lead Researcher and Developer
      I was responsible for the initial concept, design, development, and testing of the Thermal Diffusion phenomenon and the Energy Bracer VR game.
  • Problem Solver
      Addressed unique challenges related to sensory perception and feedback mechanisms by leveraging interdisciplinary research and user-centered design principles.
