Project Overview

This VR game casts players as a superhero navigating through a space prison. The objective is to escape by using ropes to move around and an energy gun to defeat enemies. The game’s unique selling point is its use of a dual haptic feedback system, which enhances the immersive experience:
  • Right-hand device
      Simulates energy gun charging using an inflatable module and provides an electrocution effect through an electrotherapy device for combat scenarios.
  • Left-hand device
      Uses a motor-driven mechanism to simulate the feeling of moving through space with a rope.

    • Challenges

  • Complex Project Collaboration
      This was my first experience working on a large-scale collaborative project. Coordinating with a diverse team posed significant challenges, particularly in aligning our goals and managing our workflows.
  • Technical Integration
      Developing seamless integration between the VR interface and the custom-built haptic feedback devices required extensive testing and adjustments to ensure real-time responsiveness and reliability.

    • Solutions

  • Effective Team Management
      I learned valuable lessons in team collaboration, which involved detailed planning and division of labor. We held regular meetings to ensure all team members were aligned with the project's progress and challenges.
  • Iterative Testing and Development
      We adopted an iterative approach to development, which allowed us to gradually integrate and refine the haptic feedback mechanisms as the project evolved. This approach helped us manage complexities in the VR and haptic feedback integration.

    • Concrete Outcomes

  • Award for Most Innovative Project
      Our project was recognized for its innovation at the Innovation in Service competition, Asia-Pacific division.
  • Enhanced Player Immersion
      The dual haptic feedback system significantly enhanced the realism and immersion of the VR escape game, leading to positive feedback from test users.
  • Skills Development
      Gained substantial experience in VR development, hardware integration, and team management, which have been instrumental in my professional growth.
